The Hidden Danger

Why Timber Rooflines Fail

Traditional properties across Lancashire were built using timber fascias and soffits. While beautiful initially, exposed wood requires constant painting and maintenance. Once the paint cracks, moisture seeps in, leading to deep structural rot.

Leaking gutters only accelerate this decay. A rotten fascia board can no longer safely support the heavy weight of water-filled guttering, eventually leading to collapse and severe water ingress into your loft space.

We provide a permanent solution. By stripping away the old, failing timber, we install robust, weather-resistant uPVC and composite boards that never need painting and will not rot, warp, or crack.

Comprehensive Upgrades

Complete Roofline Protection

We don't just replace the gutters; we rebuild the entire perimeter of your roof to ensure total weatherproofing and immaculate aesthetics.

Replacement Fascia Boards

The fascia is the primary support for your gutters and the bottom row of roof tiles. We install thick, solid replacement boards (not flimsy capping) engineered to bear heavy loads and endure decades of harsh weather.

Ventilated Soffits

Tucked under the fascia, soffits enclose the roof space. We install specialized ventilated soffits that allow steady airflow into your loft space, preventing condensation, dampness, and dry rot within your roof timbers.

Bargeboards & Apexes

Protect the gable ends of your roof. Our replacement bargeboards seal the edges of your roofline, keeping out wind-driven rain and nesting birds, while providing a crisp, clean finish to the peak of your home.

Dry Verge Systems

Traditional mortar on gable edges eventually cracks and falls out. We install mechanical dry verge systems that securely cap the edge of your roof tiles, providing a weatherproof, maintenance-free edge.

Our Strict Policy

We Never "Cap Over" Rotten Wood

Many budget installers will try to save time and money by simply nailing thin plastic capping boards directly over your existing rotten timber. This is a disastrous practice.

  • Capping seals moisture inside, rapidly accelerating the rotting process.
  • Nails driven into rotten wood provide no structural support for heavy gutters.
  • Our Method: Total removal. We strip the roofline back to the rafters, repair any damaged truss ends, and install thick, load-bearing replacement boards.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between a fascia and a soffit?

The fascia is the long, straight board that runs horizontally along the lower edge of the roof, directly behind the guttering. It supports the bottom row of roof tiles. The soffit is the board tucked underneath the fascia, bridging the gap between the house wall and the roofline, often featuring vents to allow air into the loft.

Do fascias and soffits come in different colours?

Yes. While classic gloss white remains popular, we offer a wide range of colours and woodgrain finishes including Anthracite Grey, Black Ash, Rosewood, and Golden Oak. We can perfectly match your new roofline to your property's existing windows and doors.

Do I need scaffolding for a roofline replacement?

This depends entirely on the height and access to your property. For standard two-story homes, we often use secure staging and access towers. For complex roofs, conservatories blocking access, or three-story properties, scaffolding is required for safety. We will assess this during your free site survey and include any access costs transparently in your quote.
