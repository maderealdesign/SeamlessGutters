The Hidden Dangers

A Small Leak Causes Big Problems

It is easy to ignore a dripping gutter joint during a rainstorm, but continuous water ingress is the number one cause of structural damage to UK homes. When water escapes your guttering, it runs down your brickwork, penetrates cavity walls, and rots your wooden fascias.

Left untreated, this leads to penetrating damp, black mould inside your home, and compromised roof timbers.

At Lancashire Aluminium, our repair team identifies the root cause of the problem. Whether it’s a perished rubber seal on a plastic joint, a blocked downpipe backing up, or completely rotten fascia boards dropping under weight, we provide honest, long-lasting fixes to secure your home.

Our Solutions

Comprehensive Repair Services

From quick fixes to permanent upgrades, we evaluate your roofline and recommend the most cost-effective, reliable solution.

Leaking Gutter Joints

The most common issue with traditional uPVC systems. We can reseal, re-clip, or replace damaged sections of existing guttering to stop immediate water ingress and protect your brickwork.

Rotten Fascia Replacement

If your gutters are sagging, the fascia board holding them is likely rotten. We safely remove the damaged timber, treat the rafter ends, and install solid, load-bearing replacements.

Downpipe Unblocking

Leaves, moss, and debris block downpipes, causing water to back up and overflow. We completely clear out your system, check the alignment, and can install leaf guards to prevent future build-up.

Bracket Realignment

Gutters that hold standing water will eventually bow and snap. We can adjust the 'fall' (the slope) of your existing gutters by realigning the brackets, ensuring water flows freely to the drains.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you repair my existing plastic gutters?

Yes. Even though we specialize in premium seamless aluminium, we frequently conduct repairs on standard uPVC systems. We carry replacement seals, joints, and brackets for most common profiles to get you watertight quickly.

How do I know if my fascias need replacing or just repairing?

If the timber is soft to the touch, crumbling, or causing your gutters to tilt forward, the rot has compromised the structural integrity of the board and it must be replaced. If there is just minor surface peeling, a repair and repaint may suffice. We will assess this during our free survey.

Do you charge a call-out fee for assessing a leak?

No. We offer completely free, no-obligation site assessments. We will visit your property, identify the source of the leak, and provide you with a fixed-price quote for the repair.
